About the Milky Way

The Milky Way is a barred spiral galaxy spanning over 100,000 light-years. It contains between 100 to 400 billion stars, including our Sun, and is surrounded by a massive halo of dark matter.

From Earth, it appears as a glowing band across the night sky — a result of looking inward through its dense galactic plane filled with stars, gas, and dust.

Type: Barred Spiral Galaxy

Diameter: ~100,000 light-years

Stars: 100–400 billion

Age: ~13.6 billion years

Black Hole: Sagittarius A*

Location: Local Group
